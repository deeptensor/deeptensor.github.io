The main goals and tasks of our research are the following

Development of new fundamental approaches for training, testing and storing parameters of deep neural networks. These approaches allow to reduce by orders of magnitude computational complexity and required memory for the operation of the network, while maintaining a high quality of prediction.

  1. Analysis of modern deep neural networks architectures (convolutional and recurrent neural networks, restricted Boltzmann machines, auto-encoders, etc) and identification of their connection with tensor networks.
  2. Investigation of possibilities and prospects of using various tensor networks methods for low-rank (compressed) representation of data. Development of new algorithms for tensor decompositions, including tensor train decomposition (TT-decomposition).
  3. Application of low-rank tensor approximations, in particular, tensor train decomposition (TT-decomposition) and quantized tensor train decomposition (QTT-decomposition) to deep neural networks for compressed (low-rank) representation of arrays associated with the network weights and formulation of new learning algorithms adapted to the selected low-rank approximation.
  4. Development of improved variational lower bound allowing for more accurate variational Bayesian inference. Development of stochastic optimization methods tailored to this new variational bound and study of its properties. Acceleration of constructed algorithms by low-rank tensor approximations.

Application of the developed approaches to a number of problems related to data mining and machine learning.

  1. New deep-learning methods for fast image deblurring.
  2. New deep-learning methods for fast object detection and positioning.
  3. New methods for image and video generation using machine learning and neural networks techniques.
  4. Development of mobile applications (capable of operating at low power devices such as tablets, cell phones and cameras) that utilize developed algorithms for fast image deblurring, image and video generation, object detection and positioning, etc.
